# Nepal SSF (Social Security Fund)

> ⚠️ **DISCLAIMER**: SSF rates and rules are subject to change. Verify current rules with the Social Security Fund at https://ssf.gov.np before implementation.

## What is SSF?

The Social Security Fund (SSF) is Nepal's mandatory contributory social security scheme established under the Contribution Based Social Security Act, 2074 (2017). It provides various benefits to workers in the formal sector.

## Contribution Rates

| Contributor | Rate (% of Basic Salary) |
|-------------|-------------------------|
| **Employee** | 11% |
| **Employer** | 20% |
| **Total** | 31% |

The contribution is calculated on **Basic Salary** only (not total salary).

## Contribution Breakdown

### Employee Contribution (11%)

| Fund | Rate |
|------|------|
| Provident Fund | 10% |
| Social Security | 1% |
| **Total Employee** | **11%** |

### Employer Contribution (20%)

| Fund | Rate |
|------|------|
| Provident Fund | 10% |
| Gratuity | 8.33% |
| Medical Treatment & Health Protection | 1% |
| Accident & Disability Protection | 0.67% |
| **Total Employer** | **20%** |

## Who Must Contribute?

### Mandatory for:
- All organizations with 10 or more employees
- All permanent employees in formal sector
- Contract employees with contracts > 1 year

### Exempt:
- Daily wage workers (< 1 month continuous)
- Organizations with < 10 employees (can opt-in voluntarily)
- Foreign nationals (temporary work permits)

## SSF Calculation Example

For an employee with **Basic Salary Rs. 50,000**:

| Contribution | Rate | Amount |
|--------------|------|--------|
| Employee Share | 11% | Rs. 5,500 |
| Employer Share | 20% | Rs. 10,000 |
| **Total to SSF** | 31% | **Rs. 15,500** |

### Net Salary Calculation

| Description | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Basic Salary | Rs. 50,000 |
| (-) Employee SSF @ 11% | Rs. 5,500 |
| Net Basic Paid to Employee | Rs. 44,500 |

### Cost to Employer

| Description | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Gross Salary to Employee | Rs. 50,000 |
| (+) Employer SSF @ 20% | Rs. 10,000 |
| Total Employment Cost | Rs. 60,000 |

## Journal Entries for SSF

### Recording Monthly Salary with SSF

When paying salary of Rs. 50,000 with SSF deductions:

| Account | Debit (Dr) | Credit (Cr) |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Salary Expense | 50,000 | - |
| SSF Expense (Employer) | 10,000 | - |
| SSF Payable (Employee Share) | - | 5,500 |
| SSF Payable (Employer Share) | - | 10,000 |
| Salary Payable / Bank | - | 44,500 |

### Depositing SSF to Fund

When making monthly SSF deposit:

| Account | Debit (Dr) | Credit (Cr) |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| SSF Payable (Employee Share) | 5,500 | - |
| SSF Payable (Employer Share) | 10,000 | - |
| Bank | - | 15,500 |

## SSF Deposit Timeline

| Deadline | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Monthly Deposit | Within 15th of following month |
| Late Penalty | 12% annual interest on overdue amount |

## SSF Benefits

### 1. Retirement Benefit (Pension)
- Minimum 15 years contribution required
- Monthly pension after age 60
- Lump sum option available

### 2. Medical & Health Protection
- Covers medical expenses
- Hospital treatment reimbursement
- Maternity benefits

### 3. Accident & Disability
- Compensation for work injuries
- Disability pension
- Death benefit to family

### 4. Dependent Protection
- Benefit to family on contributor's death
- Education support for children

### 5. Unemployment Benefit
- For involuntary job loss
- Minimum 3 years contribution required

## Gratuity Under SSF

| Service Period | Gratuity Rate |
|----------------|---------------|
| 1-3 years | 50% of last basic × years |
| 3-7 years | 67% of last basic × years |
| 7-15 years | 81% of last basic × years |
| 15+ years | 100% of last basic × years |

Note: For SSF members, gratuity is covered under employer's 8.33% contribution.

## Tax Treatment

### Employee Contribution
- **Tax Deductible**: Employee's 11% contribution is deductible from taxable income (up to Rs. 3,00,000/year combined with CIT)

### Employer Contribution
- **Business Expense**: Employer's 20% is fully deductible as business expense

### SSF Benefits Received
- Pension income is taxable at normal rates
- Lump sum withdrawal may have different tax treatment

## Common SSF Errors to Avoid

1. **Calculating on total salary instead of basic salary**
2. **Missing the 15th deadline** (attracts 12% interest)
3. **Not registering eligible employees**
4. **Incorrect fund breakdown** in contributions
5. **Not updating employee records** with SSF

## SSF Registration

### Employer Registration
1. Apply at SSF office with company documents
2. Get Employer SSF Number
3. Register all eligible employees

### Employee Registration
1. Fill SSF enrollment form
2. Submit citizenship/ID copy
3. Get individual SSF Member ID
