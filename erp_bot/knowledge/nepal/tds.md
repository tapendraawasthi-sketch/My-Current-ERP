# Nepal TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

> ⚠️ **DISCLAIMER**: TDS rates and thresholds change annually. Always verify current rules with IRD (Inland Revenue Department) at https://ird.gov.np before making deductions.

## What is TDS?

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is advance income tax collected at the point of payment. The payer deducts tax and deposits it to IRD on behalf of the payee.

## TDS Thresholds

TDS is NOT required if:

| Payment Type | Threshold (Single Payment) |
|--------------|---------------------------|
| Rent (Residential) | Below Rs. 25,000/month |
| Interest to Individual | Below Rs. 50,000/year |
| Service Payment | Below Rs. 50,000/transaction |

## TDS Calculation Examples

### Example 1: Professional Fee Payment

Paying Rs. 1,00,000 to a consultant with PAN:

| Description | Amount |
|-------------|--------|
| Gross Payment | Rs. 1,00,000 |
| TDS @ 15% | Rs. 15,000 |
| Net Payment to Consultant | Rs. 85,000 |

**Journal Entry:**

| Account | Debit (Dr) | Credit (Cr) |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Consultancy Expense | 1,00,000 | - |
| TDS Payable | - | 15,000 |
| Cash/Bank | - | 85,000 |

## TDS Deposit Timeline

| Deductor Type | Deposit Due Date |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Government Bodies | Same day of deduction |
| Others (with PAN) | Within 25th of following month |

## TDS Certificate

- Deductor must issue TDS certificate to payee
- Certificate must show: Amount paid, TDS deducted, PAN numbers
- Payee uses certificate to claim credit against their income tax

## Advance Tax vs TDS

| Aspect | Advance Tax | TDS |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| Who pays | Taxpayer directly | Payer deducts from payment |
| When | Quarterly installments | At time of payment |
| Rate | Based on estimated income | Fixed rates per category |

## Common TDS Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not deducting TDS for non-PAN holders at 15%**
2. **Missing deposit deadlines** (attracts 15% annual interest penalty)
3. **Incorrect rate application** (verify payment category)
4. **Not issuing TDS certificates** (required by law)
5. **Deducting from exempt payments** (e.g., below threshold)
